Innovations from the field: FHC Poothady

The NCD clinics were restarted after lockdowns were lifted in the third week of June 2021. Accordingly, FHC Poothady revamped its conduct of NCD clinic, as outlined.

Changes made:

  1. The NCD paitents of say Ward 1 will be visiting FHC Poothady on the first Monday. Similarly, NCD patients of Ward 2 will visit on first Tuesday, and so on till all the 22 odd wards to which FHC Poothady caters, are covered during the working days of the month.
  2. The First responsible person is JHI in the JHI-JPHN-ASHA Team. This JHI-JPHN-ASHA Team of the Subcentre and its wardwill present themselves at FHC Poothady on the appointed day of their ward, and be responsible for recording the BP and updating it in the IHCI cards.
  3. The fourth Saturday is earmarked for NCD patients from other panchayats
  4. After the appointed 22 days are over, patients from any ward would be seen on the remaining free days
  5. Though patients from other wards and panchayats would be seen on the days not appointed for them, preference would be for those patients from the appointed ward for that given day

Annual Review 2018 to 2020 n = 22,987 Registered Patients


Outcomes of Annual Review 2018 to 2020 & Q4 2020

For the Annual Review, all treatment cards registered since the begining of the program in 2018 till the last day of 2020 were reviewed. Subsequently the Treatment outcomes were measured in Q1 2021 for all the 22,987 patients registered in the period under review.

The keys for the Treatment Analysis are:

  1. Controlled (C) – Number of Hypertensive patients with BP less than 140/90 at their most recent visit in Q1 2021
  2. Not Controlled (NC) - Number of Hypertensive patients with BP greater than or equal to 140/90 at their most recent visit in Q1 2021
  3. Missed Visits (MV) - Number of Hypertensive patients with No visits in Q1 2021
  4. Lost to Follow Up (LTFU) - Number of Hypertensive patients who had not visited the facility for an year or more.

In addition the 510 patients registered from 1 Oct 2020 to 31 Dec 2020, were reviewed separately as the Quarter-4 Review of 2020. Similarly, the Treatment outcomes were measured in Q1 2021 for these 510 patients registered in Q3 2020. Thus, the percentages of patients with controlled BP are shown in the stacked bar graph.

PS: Mouse over the stacked bar-graph to see data for the Annual and Quarterly Reviews.



Comparison of: Q4 2020 & 2021 Monthly


Shown is the comparions of the Treatment Outcomes of Q4 2020, with the Treatment Outcomes from the Monthly Reviews during Jan 2021 to April 2021. The Missed visits had gone down from around 95% in Nov 20 to 79% in Feb 21 and to 45% in Apr 21. The biggest dip in control was in Feb 21 which however improved to 30% in Mar 21. Reduction in the COVID-19 resurgence has improved these back to the Q4 2020 levels. So, if all the visits and BP readings of the hypertensive patients must be recorded, the control rate will improve.
